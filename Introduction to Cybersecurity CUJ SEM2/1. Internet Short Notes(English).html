Introduction to the Internet

Definition: The Internet is the world's largest network, often referred to as a network of networks. It connects billions of computing devices across the globe.

It is popularly known as the "Information Highway". Unlike physical travel through geographical space, the Internet allows ideas and information to travel through "Cyberspace"â€”the electronic realm of information movement.

Key Characteristics:

  • Protocol: It uses the TCP/IP (Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol) suite to facilitate communication between devices.
  • Ownership: No single entity or individual owns the Internet. It lacks a formal centralized management organization.
  • History & Resilience: Originally developed by the Department of Defense, its decentralized structure was designed to ensure the network could survive wartime or terrorist attacks without collapsing.

Uses & The Web

Common Uses

  • Communication: Sending and receiving emails globally.
  • File Transfer: Uploading (sending) or downloading (receiving) files between computers.
  • Discussion: Participating in mailing lists and newsgroups for group discussions.
  • Surfing: Browsing the web, streaming media, and online shopping.

What is WWW?

The World Wide Web (The Web) is a collection of information organized into Web pages containing text and graphics.

Hypertext Links: Highlighted keywords or images that connect users to related information.
Website: A collection of linked web pages focused on a common theme.
Home Page: The main page of a website that serves as a central hub for navigation.

Network Types: LAN vs WAN

Feature LAN (Local Area Network) WAN (Wide Area Network)
Scope (Area) Small & confined (Room, Building, Campus) Large geographical area (Cities, Countries, Continents)
Ownership Typically Private (Home, Office, School) Private (Corporate) or Public (The Internet)
Speed/Congestion High speed, Low congestion Varies; relies on leased lines/satellites
Examples Home Wi-Fi, Office Ethernet, Computer Lab The Internet backbone, Multinational corporate networks

Key Components & Terms

The Internet

The underlying global infrastructure of connected networks and hardware.

WWW

A service running on the Internet consisting of linked documents and media.

Web Browser

Software used to access and view websites (e.g., Chrome, Firefox, Edge).

ISP

Internet Service Providers. Companies that provide access to the Internet.

Exam Cheat Sheet

1. Internet Definition

  • "Network of Networks": Links billions of devices.
  • Protocol: Uses TCP/IP for communication.
  • Origin: Developed by Dept. of Defense (decentralized to resist attack).
  • Ownership: No one owns it; no formal management organization.

2. LAN vs WAN (Crucial)

LAN Small Area (Room/Building), Private Ownership, High Speed. (e.g., Wi-Fi).
WAN Large Area (Cities/Global), Private/Public, Uses Satellites/Fiber. (e.g., Internet).

3. Vocabulary

  • Cyberspace: The electronic space where information moves.
  • WWW: The collection of linked documents (web pages).
  • Hypertext: Links that connect different pages or sections.
  • Packets: Data is broken into small chunks (packets) for travel.
