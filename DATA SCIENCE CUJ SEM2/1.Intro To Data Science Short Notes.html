2. What is Intelligence?

Intelligence is defined by the capacity to learn and solve problems. Specifically:

Core Capabilities
  • Solve novel problems.
  • Act rationally.
  • Act like humans.
  • Interact with the real world (Perceive -> Understand -> Act).
Key Functions
  • Reasoning & Planning: Modeling the external world.
  • Learning & Adaptation: Dealing with unexpected uncertainties (e.g., a baby learning to categorize animals).

Defining Artificial Intelligence (AI)

AI is a branch of Computer Science concerned with building smart machines capable of performing intelligent tasks.

"AI is a bridge between Art and Science."

Goal: Steering machines towards understanding human intelligence.

Real-Time AI Examples (Virtual Assistants):

The slides list major tech giants and their assistants:

  • Microsoft: Cortana
  • Samsung: Bixby
  • Amazon: Alexa
  • Apple: Siri
  • Google: Google Assistant

3. The Evolution of AI

The history of AI is presented as a hierarchy of subsets:

  • 1. Artificial Intelligence (1950s - 1970s):
    The broad science and engineering of making intelligent machines/programs.
  • 2. Machine Learning (1980s - 2000s):
    A subset of AI. Uses statistical methods to enable machines to improve with experience without being explicitly programmed.
  • 3. Deep Learning (2010s - Present):
    A subset of ML. Uses multi-layered neural networks to learn from vast amounts of data (The "AI Boom").

What is it? (Various Definitions)

  • Interdisciplinary: Intersection of Social Science, Statistics, Info/CS, and Design.
  • KDD: Extraction of knowledge from large volumes of structured or unstructured data. A continuation of "Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining".
  • Data Products: Not just analyzing data, but creating value/products from it (e.g., Netflix Recommendation Engine).

Data Analysis vs. Machine Learning

Data Analysis: Summarizing characteristics, visualizing, and discovering useful info.

Machine Learning: Finding patterns and building predictive models.

Intersection of Fields

Data Science sits at the center of:

  • Computer Science / IT
  • Math & Statistics
  • Domain/Business Knowledge

6. Machine Learning & Applications

Core Concept

"Field of study concerned with statistical algorithms that can effectively generalize and perform tasks without explicit instructions."

Critical Distinction

Data Science produces Insights.

Machine Learning produces Predictions.

Real-World Examples

  • Facebook: News feed algorithm (shows friends' activity based on your "likes").
  • Netflix: Predicting movie ratings for movies you haven't watched yet.
  • Fine Art: Algorithms revealing unrecognized influences between artists.
  • Q&A Sites: Voting systems where best answers rise to the top.
